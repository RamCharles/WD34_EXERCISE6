Zamboanga City

Zamboanga City in the Philippines is said to have derived its name from the Malay word "Jambangan," which means "flower paradise." Due to remnants of Hispanic influences, like the Spanish colonial buildings still standing, it is also known as "Asia's Latin City." Chavacano, a Spanish-based creole language that makes up the majority of the population, is reported to be based on 80% Spanish and the remainder on a combination of Tagalog, Hiligaynon, Portuguese, and Italian.

Dapitan City

Dapitan has been a name that almost all Filipinos are familiar of because of its importance to Philippine history. It won't be left unmentioned on every story about their national hero, Jose Rizal. But Dapitan isn't only a historic site as it stands today in the province of Zamboanga offering various sights and activities that tourists can enjoy.
